Sugar is the generic name for sweet-tasting, soluble carbohydrates, many of which are used in food. The various types of sugar are derived from different sources.<br><br>Fructose, galactose, and glucose are all simple sugars, <b>monosaccharides</b>, with the general formula C6H12O6. They have five hydroxyl groups (?OH) and a carbonyl group (C=O) and are cyclic when dissolved in water. <br><br>Lactose, maltose, and sucrose are all compound sugars, <b>disaccharides</b>, with the general formula C12H22O11. They are formed by the combination of two monosaccharide molecules with the exclusion of a molecule of water.
